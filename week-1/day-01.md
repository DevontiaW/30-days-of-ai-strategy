# Day 01: What AI Strategy Actually Means

## The Idea

AI strategy is not "use AI more." That's a wish, not a strategy. Real strategy — the Michael Porter kind — is about making deliberate choices about where to compete and how to win. Applied to AI, it means deciding which capabilities to build, which to ignore, and how AI fits into the way your organization creates value.

The biggest mistake I see? Organizations that confuse an AI project list with an AI strategy. They've got twelve pilots running, a chatbot on the website, and an "AI Center of Excellence" that nobody talks to. That's not strategy. That's activity.

## In Practice

I worked with a mid-market logistics company that had six AI initiatives running simultaneously. Predictive maintenance, route optimization, demand forecasting, document processing, customer chatbot, and a warehouse automation exploration. Six different teams, six different vendors, no shared data infrastructure.

Their "AI strategy" was a slide deck that said "become AI-driven by 2027."

We killed four of the six initiatives. Focused everything on route optimization and demand forecasting — the two that directly impacted their core margin driver (delivery cost per package). Within eight months, they had one working system in production instead of six half-built prototypes.

Strategy is choosing what NOT to do.

## Framework

**The AI Strategy Stack:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  Business Strategy              │  ← What are we trying to win at?
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│  AI Strategy                    │  ← How does AI help us win?
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│  Data Strategy                  │  ← What data do we need and how do we manage it?
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│  Technology & Talent            │  ← What capabilities do we build or buy?
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│  Governance & Ethics            │  ← How do we manage risk and maintain trust?
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

Read it top-down. If you can't clearly articulate your business strategy, your AI strategy will be random. Every layer depends on the one above it.

**Hot take:** If your AI strategy document doesn't reference your business strategy within the first page, throw it out and start over.

## Your Move

Write down your organization's top 3 strategic priorities (revenue growth, cost reduction, customer retention — whatever they actually are). Then ask: "Which of these could AI meaningfully accelerate?" If you can't draw a clear line from an AI initiative to one of those three priorities, it shouldn't be on the roadmap.
