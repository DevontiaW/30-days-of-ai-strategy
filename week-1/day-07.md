# Day 07: Week 1 Reflection + Mini-Assessment

## The Idea

You've spent six days building a foundation in AI strategy. Before moving to implementation next week, let's consolidate what you've learned, identify gaps, and check your thinking.

This isn't a test — it's a mirror. The goal is honest self-assessment so you know where to focus your energy going forward.

## Self-Assessment

Answer these five questions honestly. Write your answers down — they'll be useful input for Week 2.

### 1. Can you state your AI strategy in two sentences?

Not your company's AI project list. Your actual strategy — what AI capabilities you're choosing to invest in, what you're choosing NOT to do, and how it connects to your business strategy.

If you can't do this yet, that's fine. But it means your Week 2 priority is clarity before action.

### 2. Where does your organization sit on the data maturity scale (1-5)?

Be honest. If you scored yourself at Day 03 and the number was uncomfortable, that's useful information.

- Level 1-2: Your first "AI initiative" should be a data infrastructure project.
- Level 3: You can start AI pilots, but budget 40-60% of project time for data prep.
- Level 4-5: You're ready to move fast. Focus on use case selection and governance.

### 3. What's your #1 AI use case — and is it in the right quadrant?

Pull out the impact-feasibility matrix from Day 04. Is your current top priority a "Quick Win" (high impact + high feasibility)? Or is it somewhere else?

If it's a "Strategic Bet" (high impact, low feasibility), that's a valid choice — but make sure you also have a Quick Win running in parallel to build organizational momentum.

### 4. Do you have minimum viable governance in place?

At minimum: an inventory of AI systems, a risk classification for each, and documentation standards. If you don't have these three things, governance should be a Week 2 priority alongside your first pilot.

### 5. What surprised you this week?

This is the most important question. Surprises reveal gaps between what you thought you knew and what's actually true. Pay attention to them.

## Common Patterns After Week 1

**"We're further behind than I thought."** — Good. Accurate self-assessment is the first strategic advantage. Most organizations that think they're "doing AI" are actually doing disconnected experiments. Knowing where you actually stand is how you avoid wasting the next 12 months.

**"We need to fix our data before anything else."** — Very common. Not glamorous, but possibly the highest-ROI decision you'll make. A solid data foundation makes every future AI initiative faster and cheaper.

**"I realize we've been approaching this as a technology project, not a strategy."** — That's the point of Day 01. AI strategy is a business discipline, not a technical one. If this clicked for you this week, the rest of the series will be much more useful.

**"Our governance is basically nonexistent."** — You're not alone. Most mid-market companies have zero formal AI governance. The good news: minimum viable governance is a 2-week project, not a 6-month initiative.
